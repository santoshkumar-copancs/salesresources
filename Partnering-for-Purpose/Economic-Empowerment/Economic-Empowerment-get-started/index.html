Improving Literacy

Access to training and education leads to greater opportunities and life outcomes. Explore actions that you can take to help build access to education, equipping both individuals and communities with the resources they need to thrive and create greater opportunities for economic empowerment.
Action
Volunteer

Why it matters?

773 million adults around the world lack basic literacy skills. The ability to read is a fundamental building block to help people escape poverty and access social and economic opportunities.

Studies show that children unable to read proficiently in primary school, are much less likely to succeed in school in latter years; this blocks them from the resources and jobs to thrive in today’s increasingly knowledge-based world.

What you can do?

Team up with your chosen charitable organization to increase connectivity and create opportunity in your local community or abroad.

Additional Ideas
  • Partner with a local literacy charity
  • Organize a read-a-thon
  • Lead a book drive or contribute to tiny libraries
  • Start a pen pal program
  • Fundraise to support literacy organizations

Possible charitable organizations to get you started

  • Room to Read – Global supporting select countries
  • Chapter One – Canada, UK, US
  • World Literacy Foundationz – Global with projects in Africa, Australia, Latin America, UK, US
