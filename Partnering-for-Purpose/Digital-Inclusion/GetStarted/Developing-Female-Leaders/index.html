Developing Female Leaders

Boosting female leadership in tech is crucial to reduce workplace gender inequality and foster inclusivity.
Action
Sponsorship or nomination
Level of Complexity
Medium
Time Required
2-3 hours over 5 months
Cost
$1800 per individual
Why it matters?

There has been a slow and steady increase in the number of female leaders since the 1970s. However, today we are seeing evidence that this progress has been slowed or, in some cases, erased completely.

Womenâ€™s representation has improved across all levels of the corporate pipeline but there are persistent gaps: promotions at the first step up to manager are not equitable, and women of color lose ground in representation at every level. One in four women has considered leaving the workforce or downshifting their careers versus one in five men.

What you can do?

Commit to developing female talent by signing up to sponsor and fund a Bridge the Gap cohort. This will not only give you the opportunity to help develop women from within the industry, but also women from a charity organization of your choice.

Additional Ideas
  • Become a mentor or executive sponsor
  • Consider STEM opportunities to help attract younger female talent into the industry
